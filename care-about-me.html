Project Purpose & Goal

Menstrual Equity is defined as the affordability, accessibility, and safety of menstrual products. In the domain of this project, it is concerning students, faculty, staff, and visitors to the HCC Campus (“campus community”). Access to menstrual products is a critical element of everyday life, consistent classroom attendance, comfort during commitment to academic programs, and essential human dignity. The three pillars of the initiative are Dignity, Accessibility, and Sustainability. Dignity is the aspect of people from all cultures and backgrounds feeling welcome and comfortable in participating in this initiative. As menstruation is a taboo subject in many cultures, this project will seek to maintain professionalism in presentation, and to integrate the normality of menstruation into the background of the college campus. Dignity also leads to Accessibility, in that in order to readily access the products, patrons must feel they can do so without calling unnecessary attention to their needs, or causing excess burden or hardship. Accessibility also refers to the platform incurring as little cost as possible to the institution. Accessibility also means that this project will feature numerous different products, including some of the more difficult-to-find or costlier products. This will help ensure that the program is also Sustainable, as reusable products producing decreased landfill waste also tend to be costlier. In that, the ideal products for this project should be as plastic-free as possible, as well as being organic, and containing no bleaching agents or carcinogens, as many of the cheapest products do contain (Jin, 2018). This provision reflects the menstrual equity tenet of safety in menstrual products.

Project Scope

As part of Holyoke Community College, “HCC cares about M.E.” will work through the HCC Thrive Center to get free products to the target demographic (“patrons”). This will be done via product drives and donations from the community, as well as using earmarked funds from the HCC Foundation. The project will seek to place hospitality stations (“stations”) in prominent department lounges and offices on the HCC Campus. At launch, the project will include at least 3 stations on campus, but ideally and over time these stations will be located in each building on campus, with one station for each bathroom in any given building. They will not be located within any actual bathrooms, due to the need to adhere to ADA regulations, as well as eliminating any risk of the project interfering with the responsibilities of custodial staff. Therefore, stations will be best placed on desks, counters, or tables, in prominent offices or department lounges, for patrons to simply take as they need. The Thrive Center Director, Rosemary Fiedler, has indicated that Thrive does not have the resources to make this project a reality in the Fall 2021 Semester. This is due to staffing concerns as well as other projects that Thrive is currently committed to fulfilling in the interim. Therefore, it is appropriate to anticipate project completion in Spring 2022. At the time of the writing of this proposal, contact has been made with the Student Advisory Council of Massachusetts (“SAC”), with request to add menstrual equity to their 2021-2022 agenda. Charity groups such as No More Secrets MPS and retail groups such as DivaCup have provided donations and networking assistance to HCC. Successful outreach to the HCC student body for volunteers has been established, and opportunity for extracurricular service hours through Thrive has been offered. The focus and principles of menstrual equity indicate that the health of the menstruating body is the paramount focus. Menstrual equity does not make provisions for safe sex products such as condoms. These products will not be included in the stations. In the past, such products have been customarily distributed by Health Services, however, this campus department has been retrenched, and Thrive has not absorbed the duties or functions of Health Services. Furthermore, while human biology indicates that menstruation and reproduction are inexorably linked, it is crucial that menstruation be separated from sexual intercourse, insofar as the purpose and success of this program.

Deliverables

  • Hospitality Stations: Stations will consist of containers small enough to accommodate ADA regulations (approximately 8.5”x11” baskets or boxes). To ensure consistency of messaging, these stations will maintain uniformity and blend into the background of most departments. The stations will be kept clean and neatly organized by student volunteers (see Affected Parties), providing a wide-range of different products to accommodate diverse needs. Informational Signage: At each station, as well as in high-traffic areas on campus, informational signs (18”x22”) will describe the program, and indicate how a given individual may participate. When a patron encounters signage at a given station, the location of the particular station should be indicated.
  • Educational Literature: Stations will include informational postcards describing the program, what is offered, what other services can be obtained through Thrive, as well as a QR code and link for a campus map reflecting other station locations, and survey that will collect data for Thrive.
  • Participant Survey: Using a QR Code and/or link, a patron will access a Google form containing a brief survey about their experience with the program. This survey must be accessed through multiple modalities to accommodate patrons of differing abilities and technological access. In collecting information about the effectiveness of the program (what products are being preferred, what products are being requested, what baskets are empty or where services are otherwise lacking), the program may improve over time. As a Google form, this survey will provide visual data demonstration for the program.
  • Python Django App: Each station may include a QR code, intended to be accessed by student volunteers, otherwise the app can be accessed via browser login. This will allow for data collection on the effectiveness of the program. With the Thrive Center faculty as administrator, student volunteers will have registered accounts that will be used to access the app. Upon access, the user will be able to enter information on which products need restock, and administrators will be able to view stations are being preferred or are otherwise underutilized, as well as compiling data to demonstrate the necessity of basic needs outreach in the community. The app will afford capabilities to print weekly program data for Thrive, which will demonstrate patron involvement trends. The app will include complete instructions for future users. This app will be versatile in that it can be customized for other purposes, including use by the Thrive Food Pantry.

Demographics

It is acknowledged that not all women menstruate, and not all who menstruate identify as women. When a person lacks access to basic hygiene standards, quality of life suffers drastically. Students attending Community College often have other critical responsibilities, such as employment, children, elderly parents, and financial burdens. Too often, the basic needs of the self are addressed last, or not at all. Withholding menstrual products behind antiquated, obsolete bathroom vending machines forces a student to carry coin money at the time the need arises, which is uncommon in this era. If a student lacks this coin money, they must physically divert themselves from their purpose on the campus to access the Thrive Center. Additionally, such vending machines may have been installed during a time when gender was considered a binary principle, and some bathrooms may not have these machines installed. These factors not only pose unnecessary physical burden and inconvenience, but also calls undue attention to the action of obtaining a menstrual hygiene product. Through this program, via the Thrive Center, students will be provided with opportunities for community service. Students will commit to agreement through the Thrive Center to provide a number of service hours per week. During these service hours, the volunteers will monitor stations and signage for restock and maintenance. At this time, student leaders from the Mujeres & Non-Binary Alliance club have become invested in gaining information from the student body, insofar as desired products, and services. The leader of Mujeres, Miren Neyra Alcantara, has made contact with the makers of DivaCup (DivaCup, 2021) and secured a donation to the college, to be dispensed through Thrive when the project is running. Additionally, DivaCup has offered free training sessions for the campus community for use of the device, which is classified as a class II medical device (FDA, 2018). Additional charity donations have been sought through the menstrual equity hub “No More Secrets”, a grassroots organization based out of Pennsylvania (No More Secrets MBS, 2020).

Long-Term Goals

Due to the Thrive Center’s reliance on charitable donations, the longevity of the program must be considered. MassNOW is a grassroots program which seeks state tax funding for menstrual products in grade schools, temporary housing and relief shelters, and facilities for incarcerated individuals (MassNOW). Advocacy will take place to urge the State Legislature to account for additional, free menstrual products in higher education as well. This advocacy will take place via SAC, which has agreed to place menstrual equity in higher education on their agenda for the 2021-2022 academic year. This advocacy would aim to advocate for elimination of the barriers that students face with regard to menstrual equity.

Implementation

The goal of the 2021 Fall Semester will be to lay the groundwork for the program, including making connections with volunteers, composing the literature components, creating the hospitality stations (baskets and signs), and composing the blueprint for the API. The Python Flask Server API (Smyth, 2020) will be developed gradually with a goal of a functioning server, with registered users, by May 2022. To facilitate greatest reach, a pre-screening will be sought, via Marketing and Communications, to ascertain the campus community’s perception of need. At the end of the 2021 Fall Semester, the NSF Seminar presentation of this project will include an explanation of the understanding of basic needs insecurity as experienced by the campus community, how conjunction of effort between the volunteers and the technology (API) will improve coordination of basic needs goods for those in need, as well as presenting the informational literature, signage, and station fixtures that have been developed to date. Goals for the 2022 Spring Semester will include incorporating stations into the departments who have expressed interest, with at least three functioning stations by launch date, and deployment of the program server. By the end of the 2022 Spring Semester, this program will be a complete package, which Thrive, along with any future student leader, will be able to continue on from.
