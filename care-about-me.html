Project Purpose & Goal

Menstrual Equity is defined as the affordability, accessibility, and safety of menstrual products. In the domain of this project, it concerns students, faculty, staff, and visitors to the HCC Campus ("campus community"). Access to menstrual products is a critical element of everyday life, consistent classroom attendance, commitment to academic programs, and essential human dignity.

The three pillars of the Care About M.E. initiative are Dignity, Accessibility, and Sustainability. Dignity is the aspect of people from all cultures and backgrounds feeling welcome and comfortable in participating in this initiative. As menstruation is a taboo subject in many cultures, this project will seek to maintain the normality of menstruation into the daily activity of the college campus. Dignity leads to Accessibility, as patrons must feel they can access the products without calling unnecessary attention to their needs or causing themselves excess burden or hardship. Therefore, Accessibility also refers to the platform incurring as little cost as possible to the institution. Furthermore, Accessibility also means this project will feature a diversity of products to include diverse needs. This will help ensure that the program is also Sustainable, with reusable and biodegradable products. In that, the ideal products for this project should be as plastic-free as possible, as well as being organic, and containing no bleaching agents or carcinogens, as many of the cheapest products do contain (Jin, 2018). This provision reflects the menstrual equity tenet of safety in menstrual products.

Project Scope

As part of Holyoke Community College, "HCC cares about M.E." will work through the HCC Thrive Center to obtain free products via product drives and donations, as well as with earmarked funds from the HCC Foundation. The project will seek to place hospitality stations ("stations") in prominent department lounges and offices on the HCC Campus. At launch, the project will include at least 3 stations on campus, with the long-term goal of one station for each bathroom in any given building. Stations will not be located within the bathrooms, due to the need to adhere to ADA regulations, as well as eliminating the responsibility of the products becoming work for the custodial staff. Instead, stations will be placed on desks, counters, or tables, in prominent offices or department lounges, in a free-vend format. The Menstrual Equity Committee ("committee") has asked the Student Advisory Council of Massachusetts ("SAC") to add menstrual equity to their 2021-2022 agenda. Charity groups including No More Secrets MPS, and retail groups including DivaCup, have provided product donations and networking assistance to HCC. Student volunteers, with opportunity for extracurricular service hours through Thrive, will work to maintain the platform.

The principles of menstrual equity indicate that the health of the menstruating body is the paramount focus, therefore this platform does not make provisions for safe sex products such as condoms. While human biology indicates that menstruation and reproduction are inexorably linked, it is crucial that menstruation be separated from sexual intercourse, insofar as the purpose and success of this program. Project completion is estimated for Spring 2022.

Deliverables

  • Hospitality Stations: These 8.5"x11" containers will accommodate ADA regulations. To ensure messaging consistency the stations will be uniform in appearance and professional in presentation. The stations will be kept clean and neatly organized by student volunteers, providing a diversity of products.
  • Informational Signage: At each station and in high-traffic campus areas informational signs (18"x22") will describe the program and how to participate. Any given sign will indicate the station's location name.
  • Educational Literature: Informational postcards will describe the program, other services offered by Thrive, a QR code and link for a campus map of all station locations, and survey for participation data.
  • Participant Survey: The survey will be a Google form managed by Thrive and will gauge patron experience with the program. This survey will measure program effectiveness of the program (what products are being preferred, what products are being requested, what baskets are empty or where services are otherwise lacking) so the program may improve over time.
  • Python Django App: With the Thrive Center faculty as administrator, student volunteers will have registered accounts they will use to enter product restock information. This information will show preferred or underutilized stations, and overall success of the program. The app will be modular, and may be customized for other purposes including use by the Thrive Food Pantry.

Demographics

It is acknowledged that not all women menstruate, and not all who menstruate identify as women. When a person lacks access to basic hygiene standards, quality of life suffers drastically. Students attending Community College often have other critical responsibilities, such as employment, children, elderly parents, and financial burdens. Too often, the basic needs of the self are addressed last, or not at all. Withholding menstrual products behind antiquated, obsolete bathroom vending machines forces a student to carry coin money at the time the need arises, which is uncommon in this era. Such vending machines have been installed during a time when gender was considered a binary principle, and many bathrooms lack these expensive machines. These factors contribute to an unnecessary barrier to obtaining essential menstrual products.

Through this program, via the Thrive Center, students will be provided with opportunities for community service hours during which volunteers will monitor stations and signage for restock and maintenance. Additionally, DivaCup has offered free training sessions for use and care of their product, which is classified as a class II medical device (FDA, 2018). Additional donations have been sought through the menstrual equity hub “No More Secrets”, a grassroots organization based out of Pennsylvania (No More Secrets MBS, 2020).

Long-Term Goals

MassNOW is a grassroots program which seeks state tax funding for menstrual products in grade schools, temporary housing and relief shelters, and carceral centers (MassNOW). Advocacy through SAC will urge the MA State Legislature to account for free menstrual products in higher education institutions, for elimination of the barriers that students face with regard to menstrual equity.

Implementation

The goal of this iteration will be to lay the groundwork for the program, including making connections with volunteers, composing the literature components, creating the hospitality stations (baskets and signs), and composing the blueprint for the API. The Python Flask Server API will be developed gradually with a goal of a functioning server, with registered users, by May 2022. To facilitate greatest reach, a pre-screening will be sought, via Marketing and Communications, to ascertain the campus community's perception of need.

Future goals will include incorporating stations into the campus departments who have expressed interest, with at least three functioning stations by launch date, and deployment of the program server. By the end of the 2022 Spring Semester, this program will be a complete package, which Thrive, along with any future student leader, will be equipped to continue.
